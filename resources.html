Resources

For Teens

The Sibling Slam Book

This book is one of my favorites as it is about real teens from various ages and walks of life sharing their personal experience(s) through having a sibling with Special needs. The book is set up in a way where you are introduced to the teen and they share a bit about themselves and their sibling(s). They also have common questions that a sibling may be asked and or wondering with different answers and statements to read. The book, in my opinion, does a good job of encapsulating different opinions while being honest, yet not offensive.

Voices from the Spectrum

This book gives you different experiences from siblings, parents, grandparents, people with Autism, and professionals. In my opinion it's perfect for connecting your experiences and role of being in relation to someone on the Autism Spectrum to someone in the same scenario while also giving you different views as there are multiple different relationship roles displayed.

For Parents

Siblings of Children with Autism

This book comes from the perspectives of psychologists. This book is directed towards families but especially parents' support for their kids.

At SHOS, we know how hard it is for a parent to manage sometimes, no matter what. We encourage you to view the Sib Shop programs we have detailed as they can provide some wonderful benefits for you family and take the load off of the hard work for you.

For Kids

Autism and Me

This book is great for a reader (or listener!) from the ages 7-12. It gives real sibling experiences and a psychologist's opinion on it.

Ian's Walk

I believe this book is great for all ages of children! It acknowledges anger that a child may have towards their special needs sibling in a normal, positive way.

My Brother Sammy

This book is good for all ages. It showcases the differences that siblings might have from their sibling(s) with special needs.
